Local Delicacies of Vigan City

Walk through timeless savor authentic Ilocano cuisine, and immerse yourself in a cultural journey like no other.

Delicious food of vigan city

Vigan Empanada

A deep-fried pastry with a thin, crispy, pale crust made from rice flour. It is filled with a mixture of grated green papaya, Vigan longganisa (a garlicky and vinegary sausage), and a whole raw egg, which cooks inside the empanada. It is often eaten with sukang Iloko (Ilocano vinegar) for dipping.

Bagnet

Crispy, deep-fried pork belly, originating from the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It is prepared by first boiling the pork belly and then deep-frying it, often twice, to achieve a crispy skin and tender meat. Bagnet is typically served with sauces, such as bagoong (fermented fish paste) and Sukang Ilocos (Ilocano vinegar).

Pinakbet

Pinakbet from Vigan is a hearty vegetable stew from the Ilocos region, known for being flavored with fermented shrimp paste (\(bagoong\)) and featuring locally sourced ingredients like bagnet (crispy pork belly), tomatoes, eggplant, bitter melon, okra, squash, and long beans.

Royal Bibingka

A variety of Filipino rice cakes made of glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, eggs and cheese.

Vigan Longganisa

Garlicky, savory Filipino pork sausage from Vigan City, Ilocos Sur, known for its tangy and sour flavor, small, plump shape, and distinct yellowish color.

Poqui-poqui

The dish is often described as an eggplant hash or a deconstructed tortang talong (eggplant omelette) with a creamy and smoky flavor profile. It's a healthy, budget-friendly, and filling meal option that is high in fiber and a good source of vitamins A and C

Dinengdeng

Dinengdeng also known as inabraw is a traditional, healthy Ilocano vegetable stew from the northern Philippines. It is characterized by its use of diverse, fresh, locally-sourced vegetables in a light broth flavored prominently with bagoong isda (fermented fish paste) and typically topped with fried or grilled fish.

Sinanglao

Sinanglao is a traditional Filipino sour soup dish that originated from the Ilocos region of the Philippines, particularly associated with the city of Vigan. It is a hearty beef (sometimes pork) and beef offal soup known for its sour taste, which typically comes from kamias or tamarind, and a hint of bitterness from bile.

Vigan Pipian

Vigan pipian is a unique Ilocano porridge made from toasted ground rice, chicken, and a strong, aromatic herb called pasotes. It also includes other key ingredients like annatto for color, kamias (pias) for a sour taste, and ginger. The dish is a variant of the Mexican pipian, with the Filipino version adapted to local ingredients, and is often served as a merienda or light meal.
